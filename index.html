UN Sustainable Development Goal

No Poverty

About Goal 1: No Poverty

The first goal of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals is to end
poverty in all its froms everywhere. This goal aims to eradicate extreme poverty
and reduce overall poverty by implementing social protection
systems, promoting equal access to resources, and ensuring sustainable
economic growth.

Targets and Indicators

  • Target 1.1: By 2030, eradicate extreme poverty for all people
    everywhere, currently measured as poeple living on less than $1.25 a
    day.
  • Target 1.2: By 2030, reduce at least by half the proportion of men,
    women, and children of all ages living in poverty in all its dimensions
    according to national definitions.
  • Target 1.3: Implement nationally appropriate social protection systems
    and measurees for all, including floors, and by 2030 achieve substantial
    coverage of the poor and the vulnerable.
  • Target 1.4: Promote inclusive and sustainable economic growth, full and
    productive employment, and decent work for all as a means to eradicate
    poverty, ensuring that vulnerable groups have equitable access to
    opportunities and resources to improve their economic and social conditions.
  • Target 1.5: By 2030, build the resilience of the poor and those in
    vulnerable situations and reduce their exposure and vulnerability to
    climate-related extreme events and other economic, social, and
    environmental shocks and disasters.

Why It Matters

Poverty is a complex issue that affects billions of people around the world.
Ending poverty is not only a moral imperative but also a crucial step toward
achieving sustainable development. Poverty is often linked to lack of access
to basic needs such as education, healthcare, clean water, and economic
opportunities. Adressing poverty can contribute to reducing inequality and promoting social justice.

Get Involved

If you're intrested in contributing to the goal of No Poverty, there are many
ways to get involved. You can support organizations working to provide
education, healthcare, and economic opportunities to underserved
communities. Advocacy, volunteering, and donating to poverty-alleviation
programs are also impactful ways to make a difference.
